Banana Pudding

Discription

Banana pudding is a classic Southern dessert that combines layers of vanilla wafers, creamy vanilla pudding, and ripe bananas. Often topped with whipped cream or meringue, this sweet treat is a favorite at family gatherings and potlucks. The combination of soft pudding, crunchy wafers, and fresh bananas creates a delicious contrast in texture and flavor. Some variations include using banana-flavored pudding or adding caramel drizzle for extra sweetness.

While banana pudding is traditionally served chilled, some people enjoy it warm, fresh out of the oven with a lightly browned meringue topping. The dessert is believed to have originated in the United States in the late 19th century and has since become a staple in Southern cuisine. Many families have their own unique recipes, often passed down through generations. Whether made from scratch or using instant pudding mix, banana pudding remains a beloved comfort food for many.

This is HANDS DOWN the best banana pudding you will ever find :)

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cold milk
  • 1(5 ounce) package instant vanilla pudding mix
  • 1 (14 ounce) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1 (12 ounce) container frozen whipped topping, thawed
  • 1 (16 ounce) package vanilla wafers
  • 14 small bananas, sliced or to taste

Steps

  1. Whisk the pudding mix into the milk.
  2. Add the sweetened condensed milk and vanilla.
  3. Fold in the frozen whipped topping.
  4. Layer the mixture with the sliced bananas and vanilla wafers.
